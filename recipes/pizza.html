Classic Mumbai Pav Bhaji

Home

Description

Pav Bhaji is the ultimate Mumbai street food staple! It features a thick, spicy, mashed vegetable curry (bhaji) cooked on a flat tawa with a special blend of spices, loaded with butter, and served alongside soft, lightly toasted dinner rolls (pav).

Ingredients

  • 3 medium potatoes (boiled and peeled)
  • 1/2 cup green peas (boiled)
  • 1/2 cup cauliflower (boiled)
  • 1 large finely chopped onion
  • 2 medium finely chopped tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tablespoons Pav Bhaji Masala powder
  • 1 teaspoon Kashmiri red chili powder
  • Lots of butter (for cooking and serving)
  • Fresh cilantro and lemon wedges (for garnish)
  • 6 soft Pav buns

Steps

  1. In a large pan or tawa, melt 2 tablespoons of butter. Add the chopped onions and ginger-garlic paste, sautéing until soft and translucent.
  2. Add the finely chopped tomatoes and cook until they become mushy and start releasing oil from the sides.
  3. Add the Pav Bhaji masala, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for a minute until aromatic.
  4. Add the boiled potatoes, peas, and cauliflower. Use a potato masher to thoroughly mash all the vegetables directly in the pan until smooth.
  5. Add about 1/2 cup of water to adjust the consistency. Let it simmer on medium heat for 5 to 7 minutes, stirring occasionally. Top with a big dollop of butter and fresh cilantro.
  6. Slice the pav buns in half. Melt butter on a clean tawa, sprinkle a pinch of pav bhaji masala or cilantro, and toast the pav until soft and warm.
  7. Serve the piping hot bhaji in a plate topped with extra butter, chopped onions, a squeeze of fresh lemon, and the toasted pav on the side!
